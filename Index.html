Factoring Polynomials Flowchart

A step-by-step guide to factoring any polynomial expression.

1. START HERE

Begin with the original expression.

2. GCF?

Is there a Greatest Common Factor in ALL terms?

YES:

Factor out the GCF immediately. Work with the remaining polynomial.

NO:

Proceed to the next step.
3. TERMS?

How many terms are in the remaining polynomial?

A. TWO TERMS

  • Difference of Squares ($a^2 - b^2$)

    Formula: $(a-b)(a+b)$

  • Sum/Difference of Cubes ($a^3 \pm b^3$)

    Slogan: SOAP (Same, Opposite, Always Positive)

  • Warning:

    Sum of Squares ($a^2 + b^2$) is generally prime (cannot be factored).

B. THREE TERMS (Trinomials)

  • $a = 1$ (Simple Form $x^2 + bx + c$)

    Find two factors of $c$ that add up to $b$.

  • $a \neq 1$ ($ax^2 + bx + c$)

    Use the AC Method (split the middle term) or Trial-and-Error.

  • Perfect Square Trinomials

    Looks like: $(a \pm b)^2$

C. FOUR TERMS

  • Factoring by Grouping

    1. Group the first two and last two terms.

    2. Find the GCF of each pair.

    3. Factor out the resulting common binomial.

Check all factors for *more* factoring possibilities!
5. FULLY FACTORED?

If no step can be applied, the polynomial is factored completely.

END
